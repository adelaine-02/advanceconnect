Structured Cabling

End-to-End Network Cable Pulling (Fiber and Copper)

Installation and connection of network cables (both fiber optic and copper) in a structured cabling system, from the source device to the destination device.

Cable Harnessing and Grooming

Organization and bundling of cables in a structured cabling system to improve cable management, reduce clutter and congestion, and improve airflow and accessibility for maintenance and upgrades.

Cabling Rehabilitation

Upgrade or replacement of an existing cabling system to improve its performance, reliability, and functionality. This is done to meet changing business needs, accommodate new technology, or address deficiencies.

Continuity Testing

Testing to ensure that all components of a cabling system are properly connected and wired. It helps identify open circuits, short circuits, or other issues that may affect performance.

Fiber and Copper Termination

Attaching connectors to the ends of fiber optic and copper cables. This is a critical step that requires precision to ensure reliable and long-term connectivity.
